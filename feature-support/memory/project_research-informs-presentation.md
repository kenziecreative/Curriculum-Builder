name: Research informs curriculum presentation
description: The learning science evidence from Phase 2 gives the researcher specific language and conditions for presenting curriculum output — not just building it.
type: project
---

The Phase 2 findings don't just inform the engine's design — they give the researcher the language to present and defend curriculum decisions. Conditions like expertise-adaptive sequencing, scaffolded comparison design, and flipped classroom failure safeguards become part of how the curriculum is explained to stakeholders, not just how it's built.

**Why:** The researcher noted that the research "helps solidify the thinking around how I would present the Curriculum that comes out of this." The research has dual value: engine design AND delivery rationale.

**How to apply:** When designing the engine's output format (Phase 10), include not just the curriculum itself but the pedagogical rationale behind structural decisions — so the person presenting the curriculum has the evidence-backed "why" alongside the "what."
