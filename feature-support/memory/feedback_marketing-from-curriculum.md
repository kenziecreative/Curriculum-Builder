---
name: Marketing derives from curriculum
description: Marketing materials (promise, hook, descriptions) should be generated as output of curriculum design, not as input to it.
type: feedback
---

Marketing artifacts (promise, hook, program description, partner materials) are still needed — but they should be derived from the completed curriculum, not generated before it.

**Why:** The old template generated marketing first and backfilled curriculum to match the story. This inverted the relationship and produced marketing-heavy, pedagogically thin output. The philosophy is sound; the generation order was wrong.

**How to apply:** In the new curriculum builder's architecture, curriculum design (outcomes, sequencing, scaffolding, assessment, transfer) must complete first. Marketing/positioning becomes a downstream synthesis step that pulls from what the curriculum actually delivers. The promise is an accurate description of what the learner will be able to do. The hook is the real transformation. This applies to both tool architecture decisions (Phase 10) and any prompt design work.
