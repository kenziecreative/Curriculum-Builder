Don't over-index on competitor tool failures. The real value is encoding what Phases 1 and 2 established: scaffolding, assessment alignment, transfer design, spaced practice, expertise-adaptive sequencing, metacognitive prompts, flipped classroom safeguards.

**Why:** The researcher sees Phases 1 and 2 as the foundation for the engine's design. Phase 3 (AI landscape) is a confirmation that nobody else has solved this, not a source of design requirements. The competitive advantage is knowing what structurally sound curriculum looks like, not studying others' gaps.

**How to apply:** Keep Phase 3 as a lighter landscape scan. When designing the engine (Phase 10), draw primarily from the Phase 1 diagnosis and Phase 2 learning science evidence, not from competitor feature gaps.
