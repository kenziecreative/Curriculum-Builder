## SA-1: Cash Flow Statement Diagnostic
- **paired_objective:** MO-1-2
- **bloom_level:** Apply
- **format:** case-analysis
- **module_placement:** Module 1 / end of Session 2
- **instructions_for_learner:** You'll receive a complete cash flow statement from a fictional service business — a cleaning company with 3 employees. Read it carefully. Then complete four tasks: (1) label each of the three sections, (2) circle any line items that strike you as warning signs, (3) calculate the 90-day cash runway using the formula from today's session, and (4) write a 3–5 sentence diagnostic summary: what is this business's cash position, and what should the owner pay attention to in the next 30 days? You have 25 minutes.
- **success_criteria:** All three sections correctly labeled; at least one warning sign correctly identified with a one-sentence explanation of why it matters (if any warning signs exist in the statement); runway calculation correct within 10% of model answer; diagnostic summary addresses solvency risk in plain language and identifies at least one specific action the owner should consider.

## SA-2: Full Statement Solvency Analysis
- **paired_objective:** PO-1
- **bloom_level:** Analyze
- **format:** performance-based
- **module_placement:** Module 1 / end of Session 2 (follows SA-1)
- **instructions_for_learner:** Using the same cash flow statement from SA-1, go deeper. Write a 1-page solvency analysis that answers: (1) Which section of the statement poses the greatest risk right now — operating, investing, or financing — and why? (2) What is the relationship between this business's net income and its operating cash flow, and what does that relationship tell you? (3) What is the 90-day runway, and how confident are you in that estimate given the data quality? Your analysis should be written in plain language — as if you're explaining it to the business owner, not an accountant. You have 20 minutes.
- **success_criteria:** Analysis correctly identifies the highest-risk section with reasoning tied to specific figures; the net income / operating cash flow relationship is correctly described and interpreted; 90-day runway calculation is present and consistent with SA-1; analysis is written in plain language accessible to a non-accountant; at least three specific figures from the statement are cited.

## SA-3: Cash Management Decision Brief
- **paired_objective:** PO-2
- **bloom_level:** Evaluate
- **format:** performance-based
- **module_placement:** Program level / end of Session 4
- **instructions_for_learner:** Using your own actual cash flow statement from the most recent full month (or the provided capstone scenario if you don't have one), produce a one-page written brief. Your brief must include all four sections: (1) Cash Position — describe your current cash position and identify any warning signs; (2) Options Available — name the three short-term actions you could take and briefly note which are actually available to your business right now; (3) Decision and Rationale — select one action and defend it using at least three specific figures from your statement; (4) Solvency Projection — state how long your chosen action maintains solvency at 30, 60, and 90 days. You have 30 minutes. This brief should be written clearly enough that a business advisor, banker, or partner could read it and evaluate your reasoning without seeing your statement.
- **success_criteria:** All four sections present and substantively completed (not left partially blank); selected action defended with at least three specific figures, each identified by section and line item; 30-, 60-, and 90-day projections calculated (not estimated by feel) and internally consistent; written clearly enough for a reader without the statement to follow the logic; no prohibited reasoning ("I chose this because it feels right" — must cite data).
