## Program Outcomes

### PO-1: Learners will be able to analyze their cash flow statement to identify operating, investing, and financing cash flows and assess short-term solvency risk.
- bloom_level: Analyze
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can track income and expenses in a spreadsheet or basic accounting software; can issue invoices; recognizes when bank balance is low
- transfer_context: At their desk at month-end with their actual cash flow statement open, determining whether the business has sufficient runway to cover the next 30–90 days of obligations

### PO-2: Learners will be able to select and execute a short-term cash management action — delaying payment, accelerating collections, or drawing credit — with a written rationale.
- bloom_level: Evaluate
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can read operating cash flow totals and identify whether cash from operations is positive or negative
- transfer_context: Sitting with their cash flow statement at the moment they need to decide whether to make payroll, cover a supplier invoice, or take on a project requiring upfront expenses

## Module Outcomes

### MO-1-1: Learners will be able to describe the three sections of a cash flow statement and explain what each section reveals about business health.
- bloom_level: Understand
- parent_outcome_id: PO-1
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can recognize that a financial statement contains multiple sections; has seen a cash flow statement at least once
- transfer_context: Opening their monthly statement for the first time in a while and orienting to what each section means before reading the numbers

### MO-1-2: Learners will be able to identify warning signs in a cash flow statement, including negative operating cash flow alongside positive net income.
- bloom_level: Apply
- parent_outcome_id: PO-1
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can describe what each section of a cash flow statement contains and what it reveals
- transfer_context: Reviewing a monthly statement and flagging specific figures that signal a cash problem before the bank balance drops to zero

### MO-2-1: Learners will be able to compare the cost and risk tradeoffs of three short-term liquidity options: delaying a supplier payment, accelerating invoicing, and drawing on a line of credit.
- bloom_level: Analyze
- parent_outcome_id: PO-2
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can identify warning signs in their cash flow statement and estimate a basic cash runway
- transfer_context: Facing a cash gap and needing to weigh which option costs least, damages relationships least, and buys the most time

### MO-2-2: Learners will be able to defend a short-term cash decision using evidence from their own cash flow statement.
- bloom_level: Evaluate
- parent_outcome_id: PO-2
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can compare the tradeoffs of three liquidity options and organize them by cost, speed, and risk
- transfer_context: Explaining their cash decision to a business partner, advisor, or lender with specific numbers to back it up

## Session Outcomes

### SO-1-1-1: Learners will be able to recall the three sections of a cash flow statement and the name of each.
- bloom_level: Remember
- parent_outcome_id: MO-1-1
- prerequisite_knowledge: Has basic familiarity with financial documents; can distinguish a cash flow statement from an income statement by name
- transfer_context: Opening a cash flow statement and immediately orienting to its structure before reading any numbers

### SO-1-1-2: Learners will be able to describe what operating cash flow reveals about a business that net income alone does not.
- bloom_level: Understand
- parent_outcome_id: MO-1-1
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can name the three sections of a cash flow statement; has a basic understanding that net income is reported on the income statement
- transfer_context: Looking at two numbers that contradict each other — profit is up, but cash feels tight — and explaining why that gap exists

### SO-1-1-3: Learners will be able to explain the difference between cash-based and accrual-based accounting results using their own business context.
- bloom_level: Understand
- parent_outcome_id: MO-1-1
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can describe what operating cash flow reveals; issues invoices and is aware that payment sometimes comes later than the invoice date
- transfer_context: Reading a month where invoices were sent but not collected, and explaining to themselves or an advisor why the statement looks the way it does

### SO-1-2-1: Learners will be able to identify each line item in the operating section of a sample cash flow statement and label it as inflow or outflow.
- bloom_level: Apply
- parent_outcome_id: MO-1-2
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can describe the three sections of a cash flow statement and explain what operating cash flow reveals
- transfer_context: Working through their own monthly statement line-by-line to build a clear picture of where cash is moving

### SO-1-2-2: Learners will be able to identify the warning sign of negative operating cash flow paired with positive net income in a real statement.
- bloom_level: Apply
- parent_outcome_id: MO-1-2
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can label operating section line items as inflows or outflows; can read a net income figure from an income statement
- transfer_context: Spotting the specific pattern that signals their business may look profitable but is actually burning cash — before it becomes a crisis

### SO-1-2-3: Learners will be able to calculate a simple 90-day cash runway estimate using current operating cash flow data.
- bloom_level: Apply
- parent_outcome_id: MO-1-2
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can identify warning signs in the operating section; comfortable with basic arithmetic or spreadsheet formulas
- transfer_context: Staring at a cash position number and estimating how many weeks of operations it actually covers

### SO-2-1-1: Learners will be able to compare the short-term cash impact of delaying a supplier payment versus accelerating a client invoice using numbers from their own statement.
- bloom_level: Analyze
- parent_outcome_id: MO-2-1
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can calculate a cash runway estimate; has identified at least one cash warning sign in a real or sample statement
- transfer_context: Running two scenarios side-by-side to see which option actually moves the needle on their cash position

### SO-2-1-2: Learners will be able to differentiate when drawing a line of credit is appropriate versus when collections acceleration is the lower-cost option.
- bloom_level: Analyze
- parent_outcome_id: MO-2-1
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can compare the cash impact of delaying payment versus accelerating invoicing; has basic awareness of what a line of credit costs
- transfer_context: Choosing between two legitimate options and reasoning through cost, timing, and relationship risk in that specific moment

### SO-2-1-3: Learners will be able to organize the three liquidity options by cost, speed, and relationship risk using a decision framework.
- bloom_level: Analyze
- parent_outcome_id: MO-2-1
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can differentiate between all three short-term liquidity options and compare their tradeoffs
- transfer_context: Building a reusable mental model they can apply the next time a cash gap appears, not just the current one

### SO-2-2-1: Learners will be able to prioritize one short-term cash action given a specific scenario drawn from their own business data.
- bloom_level: Evaluate
- parent_outcome_id: MO-2-2
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can organize and compare all three liquidity options using a decision framework
- transfer_context: Sitting with a real constraint — one option is off the table, two are possible — and committing to the better one

### SO-2-2-2: Learners will be able to assess whether a proposed cash decision would maintain solvency for 30, 60, or 90 days.
- bloom_level: Evaluate
- parent_outcome_id: MO-2-2
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can prioritize a cash action from a set of options; can calculate a basic cash runway estimate
- transfer_context: Testing their chosen action against the actual runway it buys before committing to it

### SO-2-2-3: Learners will be able to defend a cash management decision in writing, citing specific figures from their cash flow statement.
- bloom_level: Evaluate
- parent_outcome_id: MO-2-2
- prerequisite_knowledge: Can assess whether a cash decision maintains solvency; can identify and reference specific figures in their own cash flow statement
- transfer_context: Writing a brief note to a business partner, lender, or advisor explaining what they decided and why — using the statement as evidence
