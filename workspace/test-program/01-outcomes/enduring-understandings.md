## Enduring Understandings

1. Profit and cash are not the same thing — a business can be profitable on paper and still run out of money, and owners who can't see the difference are flying blind.
2. A cash flow statement is a decision-making tool, not just a reporting document — it tells you whether you have enough runway to act, and that answer changes every month.
3. Every short-term cash decision has a cost, a speed, and a relationship consequence — the right choice depends on which of those constraints matters most in this moment.
4. Financial problems are rarely sudden; they leave signals in the operating section weeks before the bank account reflects them.
5. Knowing what to do with your cash flow statement is a skill that compounds — every month you practice it, the decisions get faster and more confident.
