## program_topic

Cash flow management for service-based small businesses — specifically reading cash flow statements, identifying warning signs, and making short-term decisions to maintain solvency.

## target_audience

- **description:** Owners of service-based small businesses with 1–10 employees; primarily solo operators or micro-teams in sectors like consulting, cleaning, landscaping, and personal services
- **prior_knowledge:** Can do: track income and expenses in a spreadsheet or basic accounting software, issue invoices, and recognize when the bank balance is low. Cannot yet do: read a cash flow statement, distinguish operating from financing cash flows, or project a 90-day cash position from current data
- **context_of_use:** Reviewing their own monthly cash flow statement during a quiet moment (typically end of month or when bank balance drops unexpectedly) and deciding whether to delay a supplier payment, draw on a line of credit, or accelerate collections

## transfer_context

Sitting at their desk at end of month with their cash flow statement open — the moment they need to decide whether they have enough runway to make payroll, cover a large supplier invoice, or take on a new project that requires upfront expenses.

## success_criteria

Owners can open their cash flow statement, identify the three key sections, spot a warning sign (negative operating cash flow despite positive net income), and make a concrete short-term decision (delay payment, accelerate invoicing, or draw credit line) with a written rationale.
